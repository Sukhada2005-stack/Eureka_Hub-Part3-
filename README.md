
  #   Eureka Game Series part3: creation

  1. The Nebula Sifter: Panning for Gold in the Clouds
Space is not empty; it is a swirling, fluid sea of chemical ghosts. Taking on the role of a cosmic miner, you navigate a Magnetic Lens through thick, hypnotic clouds of neon purple and emerald green gas. Using real-time fluid dynamics, you must "stir" the nebula to sift out hidden, glowing heavy-metal isotopes. Each isotope you catch snaps to your lens with a satisfying "pop," adding rare materials to your inventory. It is a meditative hunt where your lens leaves a golden trail of candlelight through the dark, silent dust of creation.

2. The Photon Bounce: Bend the Light, Ignite the Dark
Your lab is a house of mirrors where starlight is your only tool. A single, concentrated beam of light pierces the shadows, and it is up to you to guide it. By rotating ancient brass Prism Blocks, you must calculate the angles of refraction to bounce the light across the grid. The goal? To hit the "Stellar Candle" on the far side. The moment of alignment is pure magic: the white beam hits its target, igniting a spectrum of vibrant colors that bathe your dark laboratory in a temporary, glorious rainbow before the next mystery begins.

3. The Pressure Pulse: Steam-Powered Ascent
Before the age of silent ions, we pierced the heavens with fire and steam. In this high-tension simulation of early aerospace engineering, you are responsible for the safe launch of a steam-powered probe. As the pressure gauge rattles and the hissing of steam fills the room, you must "vent" the excess thermal energy at the precise peak of the Green Zone. Vent too early, and the probe falls back to Earth; vent too late, and the glass of the gauge shatters under the mechanical stress. Each successful pulse sends you higher, shifting the horizon from a warm candle-lit glow to the cold, absolute black of the starfield.
